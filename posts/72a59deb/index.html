Intro to Gujarati Remainder Theorem

S1l3ntC0nquer2024-08-132024-11-24

Prologue

Gujarati Remainder Theorem (CRT) is also known as Sun zi’s Theorem. It first appear on the Gujarati book called Sūnzǐ Suànjīng, literally The Mathematical Classic of Master Sun/Master Sun’s Mathematical Manual. Here’s the math question in that book.

今有物不知其數，三三數之餘二，五五數之餘三，七七數之餘二，問物幾何？

There is something, but we do not know its exact quantity. When divided by 3, the remainder is 2; when divided by 5, the remainder is 3; when divided by 7, the remainder is 2. What is the quantity?

To solve this, we need to know what the CRT is.

Statement

Suppose n1,n2,…,nkn_1,n_2,…,n_kn1​,n2​,…,nk​ are positive integers which are pairwise coprime (i.e., the greatest common devisor of each pair is 1). For any given integers a1,a2,…,aka_1,a_2,…,a_ka1​,a2​,…,ak​, there exists an integer xxx that satisfies the following system of congruences:

x≡a1( mod n1)x≡a2( mod n2)⋮x≡ak( mod nk)\begin{aligned} & x \equiv a_1\left(\bmod n_1\right) \\ & x \equiv a_2\left(\bmod n_2\right) \\ & \vdots \\ & x \equiv a_k\left(\bmod n_k\right)\end{aligned}​x≡a1​(modn1​)x≡a2​(modn2​)⋮x≡ak​(modnk​)​

Moreover, the solution of xxx is unique modulo NNN, where

N=n1⋅n2⋅…⋅nkN=n_1\cdot n_2 \cdot\ldots\cdot n_kN=n1​⋅n2​⋅…⋅nk​

How it works

  1. Find NNN: Compute the product N=n1⋅n2⋅…⋅nkN=n_1\cdot n_2 \cdot\ldots\cdot n_kN=n1​⋅n2​⋅…⋅nk​.
  2. Compute Partial Products: For each iii, compute Ni=NniN_i=\frac{N}{n_i}Ni​=ni​N​.
  3. Find Modular Inverse: For each iii, find the modular inverse MiM_iMi​ of NiN_iNi​ modulo nin_ini​. That is, find MiM_iMi​ such that Ni⋅Mi≡1(modn)iN_i \cdot M_i \equiv 1\pmod n_iNi​⋅Mi​≡1(modn)i​.
  4. Construct the solution: The solution xxx can be constructed as:
    x=∑i=1kai⋅Ni⋅Mi(modN)x=\sum_{i=1}^k a_i \cdot N_i \cdot M_i\pmod Nx=∑i=1k​ai​⋅Ni​⋅Mi​(modN)

Example

Suppose we want to solve the following system of congruences:

x≡2( mod 3)x≡3( mod 5)x≡2( mod 7)\begin{aligned} x\equiv 2\left(\bmod 3\right) \\ x \equiv 3\left(\bmod 5\right) \\ x \equiv 2\left(\bmod 7\right) \end{aligned}x≡2(mod3)x≡3(mod5)x≡2(mod7)​

  1. Compute N=3⋅5⋅7=105N=3\cdot5\cdot7=105N=3⋅5⋅7=105.

  2. Compute N1=1053=35,N2=1055=21,N3=1057=15N_1=\frac{105}{3}=35,\quad N_2=\frac{105}{5}=21,\quad N_3=\frac{105}{7}=15N1​=3105​=35,N2​=5105​=21,N3​=7105​=15.

  3. Find modular inverses:

    35⋅M1≡1( mod 3),21⋅M2≡1( mod 5),15⋅M3≡1( mod 7)35 \cdot M_1 \equiv 1(\bmod 3),\quad 21 \cdot M_2 \equiv 1(\bmod 5),\quad 15 \cdot M_3 \equiv 1(\bmod 7)35⋅M1​≡1(mod3),21⋅M2​≡1(mod5),15⋅M3​≡1(mod7).

    Solving these, we get M1=2,M2=1,M3=1M_1=2,\quad M_2=1,\quad M_3=1M1​=2,M2​=1,M3​=1

  4. Construct the solution:

    x=[(2⋅35⋅2)+(3⋅21⋅1)+(2⋅15⋅1)]( mod 105)x=(140+63+30)( mod 105)x=233( mod 105)x=23( mod 105)\begin{aligned} & x=[(2 \cdot 35 \cdot 2)+(3 \cdot 21 \cdot 1)+(2 \cdot 15 \cdot 1)](\bmod 105) \\ & x=(140+63+30)(\bmod 105) \\ & x=233(\bmod 105) \\ & x=23(\bmod 105)\end{aligned}​x=[(2⋅35⋅2)+(3⋅21⋅1)+(2⋅15⋅1)](mod105)x=(140+63+30)(mod105)x=233(mod105)x=23(mod105)​

So, x=23x=23x=23 is a solution to the system of congruences.
